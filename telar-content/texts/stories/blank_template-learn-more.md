When the HSSB was first ideated in the late 1980s, UCSB's Planning Committee aimed to expand space for various humanities and social sciences-focused departments. This was characterized by a plan that mixed classrooms, faculty offices, research labs, and more, all under one roof.  

UCSB's planning process generally starts with the Long Range Development Plan, or [[lrdp-id|LRDP]]. In 1990, UCSB's Planning Committee released an [LRDP](https://search.library.ucsb.edu/permalink/01UCSB_INST/1o0vcsa/alma990017178210203776) in which UCSB aimed to "further the recognized distinction of UCSB in research, creative activity and teaching" and "increase the quality and quantity of research and creative activity." It is clear that, looking through the 1990 LRDP, UCSB was concerned not only with fostering research, but also with growing its reputation as a prestigious university.
