Technical jargon doesn't describe the actual stakes at play here. For centuries, Indigenous communities watched as the federal government, professional archaeologists, and looters literally dug up their dead as well as sacred objects of their ancestors, then refused to return them. As Rebecca Tsosie argues in her article, [*Indigenous Rights and Archaeology*](https://coah-repat.com/digital-heritage/indigenous-rights-and-archaeology), "Native Americans are attempting to secure recognition of basic human rights such as the right to religious and spiritual fulfillment, and the right to control burial sites on ancestral lands." [[nagpra-id|NAGPRA]] was the first time that the US government attempted to remedy this fundamental human rights violation.
