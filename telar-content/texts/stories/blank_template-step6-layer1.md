Before the HSSB was completed in 1996, management and storage of archaeological items was disorganized, to say the least. [One 1993 report](https://apps.dtic.mil/sti/pdfs/ADA324350.pdf) notes that some remains found at the Vandenberg Air Force Base, 55 miles north of Santa Barbara, were stored in a large, open room, with cardboard boxes accessible to anyone in North Hall. It's easy to visualize the chaos of thousands of objects stored in one very large room, all without proper cataloging or management. The HSSB's underground repository (essentially a larger, more organized storage facility) would solve this problem, presumably making the repatriation process easier.
